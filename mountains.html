Aspen Mountain

Vertical Rise: 3,267 ft
Base Elevation: 7,945 ft
Summit Elevation: 11,212 ft
Skiable Acres: 675

Aspen Mountain (locally referred to as "Ajax") rises right from downtown Aspen, offering incredible views and world-class terrain. Known for its steeps, bumps, and glades, this mountain is best suited for intermediate and expert skiers and riders.

With the Silver Queen Gondola providing easy access from town, Aspen Mountain delivers 76 trails spread across 675 acres of skiable terrain, all served by 8 lifts. The mountain's rich history, dating back to 1946, and its challenging runs have made it a favorite among serious skiers for generations.

Snowmass

Vertical Rise: 4,406 ft
Base Elevation: 8,104 ft
Summit Elevation: 12,510 ft
Skiable Acres: 3,342

Snowmass is the largest of the four mountains, offering an incredible variety of terrain across its 3,342 acres. With the greatest vertical drop in the United States, Snowmass provides long, uninterrupted runs and diverse terrain features that appeal to skiers and riders of all abilities.

The mountain is particularly well-suited for families, with excellent children's facilities, terrain parks, and gentle learning areas. Advanced skiers will find challenging terrain in the Cirque and the Hanging Valley Headwall, while intermediate skiers can enjoy the vast network of groomed cruisers.

Aspen Highlands

Vertical Rise: 3,635 ft
Base Elevation: 8,040 ft
Summit Elevation: 11,675 ft
Skiable Acres: 1,040

Aspen Highlands is a local favorite, known for its uncrowded slopes, spectacular views of the Maroon Bells, and the legendary Highland Bowl. The Bowl, accessible by a hike from the top of the Loge Peak lift, offers some of the most challenging in-bounds terrain in North America.

Beyond the Bowl, Highlands features long, consistently pitched cruisers and some excellent intermediate terrain. With 1,040 acres of skiable terrain and 122 trails, this mountain provides a more peaceful experience compared to its neighbors, allowing you to truly connect with the mountains.

Buttermilk

Vertical Rise: 2,030 ft
Base Elevation: 7,870 ft
Summit Elevation: 9,900 ft
Skiable Acres: 470

Buttermilk is a mountain of contrasts. Best known as the long-time home of the Winter X Games, it features world-class terrain parks and a 22-foot superpipe. Yet it's also the perfect mountain for beginners and families, with gentle, well-groomed slopes and excellent learning areas.

The mountain's 470 acres include 44 miles of trails and 8 lifts. The Hideout, Buttermilk's innovative children's center, makes this mountain particularly appealing for families with young children just learning to ski or ride. Meanwhile, Tiehack on the mountain's east side offers some surprisingly challenging terrain for more advanced skiers.
